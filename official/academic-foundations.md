# Academic Foundations of Prediction Markets

## Seminal Papers

### Market Design & Efficiency
- **Hayek (1945)** — "The Use of Knowledge in Society" — The foundational argument for markets as information aggregation mechanisms
- **Arrow & Debreu (1954)** — Complete markets theory — the theoretical basis for event contracts
- **Hanson (2003)** — "Combinatorial Information Market Design" — LMSR (Logarithmic Market Scoring Rule), the AMM used by early prediction markets
- **Wolfers & Zitzewitz (2004)** — "Prediction Markets" — The Journal of Economic Perspectives survey that legitimized the field

### Calibration & Accuracy
- **Brier (1950)** — "Verification of Forecasts Expressed in Terms of Probability" — The Brier score, still the standard calibration metric
- **Tetlock (2005)** — "Expert Political Judgment" — Why prediction markets beat pundits: foxes vs hedgehogs
- **Tetlock & Gardner (2015)** — "Superforecasting" — How the best forecasters think, and what markets can learn from them
- **Page (2007)** — "The Difference" — Diversity prediction theorem: why crowds are wise

### Market Microstructure Applied to Events
- **Manski (2006)** — "Interpreting the Predictions of Prediction Markets" — Why market prices aren't pure probabilities (risk premia, favorite-longshot bias)
- **Fountain & Harrison (2011)** — "What Do Prediction Markets Predict?" — Empirical analysis of prediction market accuracy
- **Rothschild (2009)** — "Forecasting Elections: Comparing Prediction Markets, Polls, and Their Biases" — Head-to-head accuracy comparison

### Mechanism Design
- **Budish, Cramton & Shim (2015)** — "The High-Frequency Trading Arms Race" — Why continuous trading creates problems (relevant to PM microstructure)
- **Chen & Pennock (2007)** — "A Utility Framework for Bounded-Loss Market Makers" — Generalized scoring rules for market making

## Textbooks & Books
- **Abramowicz (2007)** — "Predictocracy: Market Mechanisms for Public and Private Decision Making"
- **Sunstein (2006)** — "Infotopia: How Many Minds Produce Knowledge"
- **Arrow et al. (2008)** — "The Promise of Prediction Markets" — Science magazine manifesto signed by 22 economists

## Working Papers & Recent Research
- **Gjerstad (2004)** — "Risk Aversion, Beliefs, and Prediction Market Equilibrium"
- **Berg, Nelson & Rietz (2008)** — "Prediction Market Accuracy in the Long Run" — Iowa Electronic Markets vs polls over 20 years
- **Snowberg, Wolfers & Zitzewitz (2012)** — "Prediction Markets for Economic Forecasting" — Handbook chapter on PM methodology
