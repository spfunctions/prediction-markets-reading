## Market Overview

Trading activity in Week 52 was dominated by markets with imminent, year-end resolutions. Total volume surged as traders positioned on final 2025 outcomes, creating a clear divergence: high conviction on monetary policy and low conviction on both crypto rallies and political surprises. The flat 50% price on the Trump exit question absorbed nearly $10M in volume alone, indicating a market deeply divided or hedging significant exposure.

## Key Highlights

## Desk Breakdown

### Elections Desk ⚪

All eyes on the binary, high-volume 'Trump Out This Year?' market, which traded at a perfectly efficient 50/50 through the week. The 2028 Presidential Election market remained active as a longer-duration hedge.

### Macro & Rates Desk 🟢

Extreme conviction in a third Fed rate cut for 2025 (98% YES). The 'Powell Leaves Before 2026?' market stayed dormant at 1%, indicating strong confidence in continuity at the Fed. The '2 Cuts' market traded at just 6%, showing the market views three cuts as the base case.

### Policy & Tech Desk / Crypto 🔴

Crypto markets dominated volume but showed fading bullish momentum for 2025. The chance of Bitcoin reaching $100k fell to 11%, while 'how high' markets for both BTC and ETH traded in the 1-2% range. The 'how low' Bitcoin market at 20% suggests more concern about downside than upside in the final days of the year.

### Geopolitics Desk ⚪

The Ukraine-Russia Conflict 2025 market saw steady, lower-volume activity. No major probability shifts were noted, suggesting a stalemate priced into year-end.
